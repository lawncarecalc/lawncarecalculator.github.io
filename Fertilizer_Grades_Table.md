# Help Me Choose a Fertilizer at the Garden Center — Common Grades Table

This is the brand-free reference table now built into the calculator's "Help me choose a fertilizer at the garden center" section. Residents match the N-P-K and WIN on any bag to the closest row — an exact match isn't needed, just something close. Generic profiles reflect what is commonly stocked at Virginia garden centers; no brands are endorsed.

## Common grades at a glance

| Grade (N-P-K) | Best use | Typical WIN (% of total N) | Program |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **10-10-10** | P & K deficient soil, establishment | 0% | P1 |
| **16-4-8** | Moderate P need, general maintenance | 0% | P1 |
| **5-10-5** | Low N, starter / first application | 0% | P1 |
| **28-0-3 to 32-0-4** | P adequate/high, standard maintenance (most common big-box zero-P grade) | 0–14% | P1 |
| **29-0-3 to 38-0-4** | P adequate/high, low K, maintenance | 0–14% | P1 |
| **46-0-0** (urea) | P & K adequate, N correction only | 0% | P1 |
| **25-0-5** (methylene-urea / MESA type) | Zero-P maintenance, fewer applications | ~50% | P3 |
| **32-0-10** (poly-coated urea) | Zero-P maintenance, slow feed | 50–65% | P3 |
| **39-0-0** (ureaform) | Zero-P, N-only, longest feed | ~70%+ | P3 |
| **9-0-9** (organic, feather/blood meal) | Zero-P organic maintenance | ~85%+ | P3 |
| **Any slow-release blend** (WIN 15–49% of N on label) | Any situation — fewer applications, lower burn risk | 15–49% | P2 |

## How to read WIN correctly

⚠️ **Program is set by WIN as a percent of total nitrogen, not percent of bag weight.** Divide the label's WIN figure by the total N%.

- Example: a bag listing 24% total N and "0.6% water insoluble nitrogen" → 0.6 ÷ 24 = **2.5% of N** → Program 1.
- Example: a 39-0-0 listing "28.5% water insoluble nitrogen" → 28.5 ÷ 39 = **73% of N** → high-WIN slow release.

**Slow-release chemistries to look for in the ingredient list:** methylene urea, MESA, ureaform, sulfur- or polymer-coated urea, IBDU, or protein-based organics (feather/blood/meat meal).

## Program thresholds (VCE Soil Test Notes 17 & 18)

- **WIN < 15% of N → Program 1** (quick-release): max 0.7 lb N/1,000 sq ft per application.
- **WIN ≥ 15% of N → Program 2** (slow-release): max 0.9 lb (cool-season) / 1.0 lb (warm-season) per application.
- **Program 3** in the calculator covers the highest-WIN products (≥50%).

## Important cautions

- **"Slow-release," "controlled-release," "slow-feed formula," and "contains X% organic fertilizer" are NOT the 15% test.** Only the WIN line in the guaranteed analysis is reliable. *(VCE 430-011)*
- **Phosphorus-containing products are excluded from the zero-P maintenance rows** by design. When soil tests High/Very-High for P, choose a zero-P grade to prevent runoff. (Note: many organic N sources and all "Starter" formulas carry phosphorus — starter grades like 24-25-4 or 21-22-4 are for new lawns only.)
- **Formulations change yearly.** Treat these as starting profiles; the bag's guaranteed-analysis label is always the authority.

*Sources: VCE 430-011; VCE Soil Test Note 17 (452-717) and Note 18 (452-718). No brands endorsed.*
